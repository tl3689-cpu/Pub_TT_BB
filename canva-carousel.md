Create a premium, professionally designed LinkedIn carousel based on the following slide content.

STYLE & QUALITY:
The design should feel like it was created by a world-class brand designer for a senior business advisory. Modern, minimal, premium, clean, high-contrast, swipe-friendly. Understated authority over hype. Avoid generic Canva templates. Each slide should make the reader want to swipe to the next.

BRAND COLOURS (Efficient Business Solutions):
- Deep Teal: #0F6E56 (primary brand presence — cover accent, slide-number badges, the underline on slide titles, thin rules and dividers; not for body text)
- Signal Amber: #BA7517 (the single action colour — CTA slide background only; used once, never for body text or decoration)
- Ink Charcoal: #16302B (dark cover background, and near-black body text on light slides)
- Warm Paper: #F7F5EF (light body-slide background, and text on dark or amber slides)
- Slate Teal: #5E736B (secondary text, slide indicators, mono labels, sub-captions on light slides)
- Subtle paper-grid texture on light slides, very low opacity

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Slide titles: Playfair Display, large (60-80pt), tight tracking
- Slide body: Poppins, medium (28-32pt), generous line height
- Slide numbers + labels: DM Mono, small (16-20pt)

LAYOUT REQUIREMENTS (per slide):
- **Slide 1, cover:** Ink Charcoal background (#16302B), Warm Paper title, Deep Teal accent (a thin line, a small badge, or one keyword in teal). Author name and handle bottom-left in DM Mono, Slate Teal.
- **Slides 2 to N-1, body:** Warm Paper background (#F7F5EF), Ink Charcoal title at top with a 3px Deep Teal underline, body text below in Ink Charcoal, secondary text in Slate Teal, a small "[N/Total]" indicator top-right in Deep Teal.
- **Slide N, CTA:** Signal Amber background (#BA7517), Ink Charcoal title with the call to action ("Start the BORe Diagnostic", "Comment X", "Save and share"), author handle bottom-left in Ink Charcoal DM Mono. This is the only amber in the deck — let it stand out.
- All slides: 1080x1350 vertical format, generous margins, perfect alignment.

DESIGN DIRECTION:
- Editorial-style composition, like a business magazine spread
- Strong typographic hierarchy, slide titles 3-4x the body size
- Minimal but high-contrast
- Each slide must work as a standalone screenshot
- Cohesive visual rhythm slide to slide, consistent design language

ICONS & ILLUSTRATIONS:
- Optional: a thin-line custom icon top-right of each body slide reinforcing the slide topic
- Icons cohesive, premium, modern, thin-line, in Deep Teal or Ink Charcoal
- One consistent illustration style throughout
- Never decorative for its own sake — every icon must mean something

TEXT STYLING:
- Important keywords in bold (never in colour; the teal accent carries emphasis)
- Slide titles always short (max 8 words)
- Body text always short (max 2 lines per slide — this is a carousel, not a cheatsheet)
- Excellent readability on mobile

OUTPUT STYLE:
The final carousel should feel like a mix of:
- Apple keynote slides (high contrast, big type)
- Monocle magazine spreads (editorial composition)
- A senior consulting deck (composed, credible, uncluttered)
- A Vercel landing page (clean, technical, minimal)

OPTIONAL ENHANCEMENTS:
- Subtle texture overlays on light slides
- Soft shadows under text blocks
- Thin Slate Teal line dividers between sections within a slide
- Premium UI-inspired elements (corner brackets, fine rules, slide-number badges in Deep Teal)

FORMAT:
- Vertical, 1080x1350 per slide (LinkedIn-optimal)
- 5-7 slides total (per the input)
- High resolution, crisp typography, consistent visual rhythm

## Tips for the best output

1. **Run the prompt as-is the first time.** Canva AI gets the layout right ~70% of the time on the first pass.
2. **If slides feel inconsistent:** ask Canva "make all body slides use the same template, only swap the title and body text."
3. **If the dark cover feels too heavy:** ask Canva "soften the Ink Charcoal cover to a deep teal-grey, keep the teal accents."
4. **If text is too small on mobile:** ask Canva "increase slide title to 80pt and body to 32pt, this is for mobile reading."
5. **Save as a Canva template** the first time. Every future carousel uses the same template, just swap the copy.
6. **For long carousels (7+ slides):** consider splitting into a 2-part series. Engagement drops past slide 7.
