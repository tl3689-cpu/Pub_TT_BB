## ONE AVATAR: The Exposed Leader

They run, or help run, a founder-led professional or financial services firm between $10M and $100M, with 25 to 250 staff. From the outside it looks successful. Inside, the growth has outpaced the structure. Risk is managed in silos, the numbers are not fully trusted, key functions depend on individuals, and disconnected tools drain margin. They sense the exposure but cannot see the whole picture, because the firm has never had an enterprise architect.

**The turning point trigger:**
A near-miss, a board or audit question, or the quiet realisation that one resignation or one incident would tip the firm into crisis.

**Their internal monologue:**
"We are bigger than we were, and somehow more fragile. Risk lives in the gaps between my teams, and no single person sees the whole thing. If the board asked me today how fast we could recover, I am not sure I could answer. I do not need another tool. I need someone who can see the entire structure."
