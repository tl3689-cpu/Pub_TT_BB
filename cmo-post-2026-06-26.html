Your business has a third shift. Nobody works it. Your people give you 8 hours. Your customers want 24, 7 days a week. That gap, nights and weekends, is where the fragility lives. (Save this if your business still stops the moment you leave the room ♻️) Operational fragility always hides in the same place: the work only you can do. Here is how the strongest COOs, CFOs and CEOs close it: 1. Map every task only one person can do. That list is your real risk register. 2. A messy process automated is just a faster mess. Write it down before you touch a tool. 3. The 24/7 work belongs on a system. A hero burns out. Software never misses a weekend. 4. Translate every fix into ROI. 30 minutes saved across 80 people is real money back. 5. Hand your people the system, then give them the harder, more valuable work. That is a system that empowers your people. 6. People, process, technology, in that order. Buying the tech first is why most systems sit unused. 7. Build for the day you are not in the room. If it breaks without you, it was never a system. A business that runs without you is not luck. It gets built step by step, step by step. If you want the people, process, technology checklist I use to find those single points of failure, comment SYSTEM. P.S. The server refresh that finished six months early ran on a system, not on me.
