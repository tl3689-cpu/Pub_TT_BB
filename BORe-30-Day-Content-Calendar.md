## HOW THIS WORKS

Five posts a week, Monday to Friday, twenty posts across the month. Weekends are for
engagement, not posting. Each day below is a brief, not a finished post. On the day, paste the
brief into the BORe Operator and run `POST:`, then run the Canva prompt to make the visual.

Three things keep it from repeating and make it convert:

1. **A rotation matrix.** Three pillars by five awareness stages by several formats and two
   running series. The same idea never lands twice because the angle, the stage and the format
   all move.
2. **A CTA rhythm.** Most posts carry no ask. Roughly one in six invites the BORe Diagnostic,
   and a few offer a free lead magnet as the low-friction first yes. Value earns the ask.
3. **A bridge.** Every post points, directly or through the profile, toward the diagnostic or
   a free asset that leads there.

**The pillars:** P1 Operational Fragility as Commercial Risk · P2 The Enterprise Architect Gap
· P3 Resilience Over Growth.
**The series:** Five Structural Risks (one risk dissected at a time) · Structural Failure
Dissected (a public operational failure read through the five risks, renewable forever).
**CTA legend:** NONE (end on a question) · SOFT (invite a comment or DM) · MAGNET (free asset,
email capture) · BORe (explicit diagnostic ask).

## THE CONVERSION SYSTEM — LEAD MAGNET, STEP BY STEP

### Which asset is the first yes
Lead cold inbound with the **Operational Readiness Checklist**. It is the lowest-friction yes,
a quick practical scan a leader will trade an email for in seconds. The eBook, *From Fragile
Success to Durable Control*, is the mid-funnel asset for prospects who have already named the
pain. The path runs: checklist, then eBook, then BORe Diagnostic, then Review. Low commitment
first, deeper commitment as trust builds.

### The wiring, on your current stack (HubSpot Starter, Gmail, the two sites)

**Step 1 — Build the capture.** In HubSpot, create a form with two fields only, first name and
work email. Fewer fields, more completions. Put it on a simple landing page, either a new
page on efficientbusiness.solutions or a HubSpot landing page. The headline names the exposure
the checklist reveals. One button: "Send me the checklist."

**Step 2 — Create the contact properties.** On the HubSpot contact, set Lead Source to the
magnet name, ICP Score to be filled later by the operator, and Warm-Up Stage to "magnet
downloaded." This is why Starter was worth the upgrade.

**Step 3 — Deliver instantly.** The form triggers an automatic delivery email with the
checklist attached or linked. Subject: "Your Operational Readiness Checklist." Body: warm, one
line on how to use it, and a soft line that the full picture is the free BORe Diagnostic. No
hard sell on the first touch.

**Step 4 — Run a 5-touch nurture over twelve days.** Use the newsletter-writer skill to draft
each in your voice. The sequence:

- Email 0, instant: deliver the checklist. CTA soft, mention the diagnostic once.
- Email 1, day 2: one of the five risks told as a short story, the cost of leaving it unseen.
  CTA none.
- Email 2, day 5: offer the eBook as the deeper read. CTA download the eBook.
- Email 3, day 8: the board recovery question, resolved by the diagnostic. CTA take the BORe
  Diagnostic.
- Email 4, day 12: one proof point, the manufacturer record or the IBM pedigree, and an
  invitation to the free 45-minute Review. CTA book the Review.

**Step 5 — Measure per magnet.** Tag every link with a UTM so HubSpot shows which post and which
magnet produced a download, a diagnostic, and a Review. Watch diagnostics started and Reviews
booked, not opens.

**Step 6 — Close the loop to the skill.** When a nurtured contact replies or books, the operator
runs `REPLY:` and `LOG:`, and the human, qualified conversation begins. The content fed the
machine, the machine fed the calendar, the calendar fed the diagnostic.

### Why this converts
A cold COO will not jump from a post to a diagnostic. They will take a free checklist, read a
useful email or two, and book the week a near-miss or a board question makes the timing real.
The magnet buys you the email, the nurture buys you the trust, the diagnostic buys you the
conversation. Patience is the strategy.

There is a question every board will ask in the next two years, and most leaders cannot answer
it cleanly.

How fast can we recover.

Not how fast did we grow. How fast do we recover when a key person leaves, when a system goes
down, when an audit finds the gap nobody owned.

I watched a firm answer that question the hard way. One person held the whole billing process in
their head. They resigned on a Friday. By the following Wednesday the firm could not invoice,

The fragility was not new. It had been sitting there for years, in the gaps between teams, where
no single person saw the whole thing. Nobody had measured it.

That is the work. Read the business at 360 degrees across people, process, technology and risk,
the same way an enterprise architect reads a $100M system, then translate it into one operation
a non-technical leader can run.

You cannot fix what you have never measured.

So measure it. The BORe Diagnostic is free, it takes a few minutes, and it gives you an instant
read on how exposed your firm really is, across all five structural risks.

If the board asked you today, you would have the answer.

**Why it works:** it opens on a situation, not a statistic. It grounds the principle in a real

## THE OPERATING RHYTHM

- **Daily:** run `POST:` against the day's brief, generate the Canva visual, publish. Run
  `COMMENT:` on five to eight ICP posts. Reply to every comment on your own posts within the hour.
- **Weekly:** review which posts produced diagnostics and Reviews, not likes. Keep the angles
  that convert, drop the ones that only applaud.
- **Fortnightly:** refresh the two series from the week's headlines and your own deals, so the
  calendar never runs dry.
- **Monthly:** rebuild the next 30 days from the angles that worked, weighting the winners.

The calendar is the blueprint. The skill is the builder. The magnet and the diagnostic are the
loading dock. Together they turn daily content into a pipeline.
